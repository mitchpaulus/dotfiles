Yes. The easiest field cues are the **keypad shape, case styling, and overall industrial design era**.

**ACH400**

* Usually looks the most dated: a **rounded, beige/tan enclosure** with a **small dark display window** and a narrow keypad area. Many units have a more “curved shell” look than later ABB drives. ([ABB Library][1])

**ACH550**

* Still light gray/beige, but more modern than the ACH400.
* Typically has ABB’s older **Assistant Control Panel** look: a **rectangular screen** with **simple arrow keys** underneath, and a more angular front cover.
* It often has a visible **blue HVAC badge** near the top/front. ABB’s ACH550 manuals and product literature identify it as the HVAC-specific 550 generation. ([ABB Library][2])

**ACH580**

* Usually the easiest to spot as the newest family.
* More modern front face with a **larger graphical keypad**, a **distinct curved silver/gray bezel** around the control panel, and a cleaner cabinet style.
* ABB’s current ACH580 manuals are still actively updated, which also matches its position as the current HVAC family. ([ABB Search][3])

A practical shorthand:

* **ACH400** = rounded, older beige body
* **ACH550** = boxier beige/gray body with older rectangular keypad
* **ACH580** = newer-looking gray/white body with the more refined, framed keypad and cleaner front layout ([ABB Library][1])

Two caveats:

* **Enclosure type matters.** Wall-mounted, cabinet-built, and module versions can look pretty different even within the same family. ([ABB Library][2])
* **Keypads can be swapped** in some installations, so the keypad alone is not a perfect identifier. The safest check is still the **nameplate/model code** on the side or front. ABB’s manuals identify families by codes like **ACH550-01** and **ACH580-01**. ([ABB Library][2])

The most reliable visual tell in the field is usually the **control panel generation**:

* ACH400: small, older dark window
* ACH550: older rectangular LCD + simple arrow pad
* ACH580: newer framed graphical panel with a more polished bezel
